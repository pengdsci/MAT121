📊 Normal Tests of Means & Proportions: P-value & Critical Value

📋 6‑Step Procedure recall

1. Identify claim (parameter: μ or p)
2. Set $H_0$ and $H_a$
3. Compute test statistic
4. Find critical value(s) / p‑value
5. Decision: reject or fail to reject $H_0$
6. Conclusion about the claim.

$TS = \frac{\bar{x} - \mu_0}{s/\sqrt{n}}$   (mean)
$TS = \frac{\hat{p} - p_0}{\sqrt{p_0(1-p_0)/n}}$   (proportion)
reviewed 6‑step
🎯 p‑value definition

Right‑tailed: area to the right of $TS$
Left‑tailed: area to the left of $TS$
Two‑tailed: $2 \times$ smaller tail area

if p‑value $< \alpha$ → reject $H_0$
if p‑value $> \alpha$ → fail to reject $H_0$

Decision rule equivalent to critical‑value method.

p‑value method
📈 Mean test z‑test

Test $H_0: \mu = \mu_0$ (or $\le$, $\ge$)
Test statistic:

$Z = \frac{\bar{x} - \mu_0}{s / \sqrt{n}} \sim N(0,1)$

Conditions: $n \ge 30$ (CLT) or normal population.

Example: $n=200,\; \bar{x}=51750,\; s=5240,\; \mu_0=50352$
$Z = 3.77$ → reject $H_0$ at $\alpha=0.05$.

mean z‑test
📊 Proportion test z‑test

Test $H_0: p = p_0$ (or $\le$, $\ge$)
Test statistic:

$Z = \frac{\hat{p} - p_0}{\sqrt{p_0(1-p_0)/n}} \sim N(0,1)$

Conditions: $n p_0 > 5$ and $n(1-p_0) > 5$.

Example: $n=166,\; \hat{p}=0.73,\; p_0=0.80$
$Z = -2.255$, p‑value $= 0.0122$ → reject $H_0$.

proportion z‑test
⚖️ CV vs p‑value decision

Critical value: compare $TS$ to $z_{\alpha}$ (or $z_{\alpha/2}$).
p‑value: compare area to $\alpha$.

Right‑tailed: reject if $TS > z_{\alpha}$
Left‑tailed: reject if $TS < -z_{\alpha}$
Two‑tailed: reject if $|TS| > z_{\alpha/2}$

Both methods lead to same conclusion.

CV & p‑value
🧪 Mean example p‑value

Claim: machine should be stopped if $\mu \ne 8$ oz.
$n=36,\; \bar{x}=7.91,\; s=0.2$ (variance $0.04$)
$H_0: \mu=8$ vs $H_a: \mu \ne 8$ (two‑tailed)

$TS = \frac{7.91-8}{0.2/\sqrt{36}} = -2.7$
p‑value $= 2 \times 0.0035 = 0.007 < 0.05$ → reject $H_0$.

Conclusion: machine should be stopped.

mean example
🧪 Proportion example left‑tailed

Claim: at least 80% satisfied ($p \ge 0.8$).
$n=166,\; \hat{p}=0.73,\; p_0=0.80$
$H_0: p \ge 0.8$ vs $H_a: p < 0.8$

$TS = \frac{0.73-0.80}{\sqrt{0.80(0.20)/166}} = -2.255$
p‑value $= 0.0122 < 0.05$ → reject $H_0$.

Evidence supports CEO’s claim.

proportion example
📐 Conditions CLT

Mean test: $n \ge 30$ (or normal population).
Proportion test: $n p_0 > 5$ and $n(1-p_0) > 5$.

$\bar{x} \sim N(\mu, \sigma/\sqrt{n})$
$\hat{p} \sim N(p, \sqrt{p(1-p)/n})$

Both test statistics follow standard normal under $H_0$.
