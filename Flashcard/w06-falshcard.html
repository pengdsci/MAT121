📊 Confidence Intervals for Means

🧩 Framework & terms 1/8

Point estimate: single value from sample (e.g. $\bar{x}$ for $\mu$).

Bias: $\bar{x} - \mu$  |  Variance: measures precision.

$ \bar{X} \sim N(\mu,\; \sigma/\sqrt{n}) $   (CLT, $n>30$)

Confidence level $1-\alpha$ = middle area; $\alpha$ = total tail area.

Critical value (CV): $z_{\alpha/2}$ (e.g. 1.96 for 95%).

I understand the sampling distribution of $\bar{x}$
📐 Formula & E 2/8

Margin of error: $E = CV \times \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}$

$ \bar{x} \pm E \quad \Rightarrow \quad (\bar{x}-E,\; \bar{x}+E) $

Interpretation: "We are $95\%$ confident that the interval contains $\mu$."

width = $2E$  ·  $n \uparrow \Rightarrow E \downarrow$  ·  confidence $\uparrow \Rightarrow E \uparrow$

I can compute $E$ and build the CI
📋 5‑Step procedure 3/8

Example: $n=81,\; \bar{x}=57,\; s=15$, 95% CI.

  1. Confidence: $1-\alpha=0.95 \Rightarrow \alpha/2=0.025$
  2. CV: $z_{0.025}=1.96$
  3. E: $1.96 \times 15/\sqrt{81} = 3.27$
  4. CI: $57 \pm 3.27 = (53.73,\; 60.27)$
  5. Interpret: "95% confident $\mu$ lies in (53.73, 60.27)"
I can apply all 5 steps
🎯 Critical values 4/8

Common CVs (two‑tailed):

$90\% \rightarrow z_{0.05}=1.645$
$95\% \rightarrow z_{0.025}=1.96$
$99\% \rightarrow z_{0.005}=2.576$

As confidence $\uparrow$, CV $\uparrow$, interval $\uparrow$ (wider).

For 99%: $E = 2.576 \times s/\sqrt{n}$.

I remember the critical values for 90%, 95%, 99%
🐟 Walleye example 5/8

$n=100,\; \bar{x}=7.5,\; s=2.3$, 95% CI.

$E = 1.96 \times \frac{2.3}{\sqrt{100}} = 0.451$
$CI = 7.5 \pm 0.451 = (7.05,\; 7.95)$

Interpret: 95% chance interval contains true mean length.

✔️ CLT applies because $n>30$.

I understand the walleye CI
⚖️ Bias & variance 6/8

Bias = accuracy (ideal: 0).

Variance = precision (smaller = better).

$ \text{Bias}(\bar{x}) = \bar{x} - \mu $
$ \text{Var}(\bar{x}) = \sigma^2 / n $

Good estimator: unbiased and low variance.

CLT ensures $\bar{x}$ is approximately unbiased.

I grasp bias vs. precision
📊 Sampling distribution 7/8

From $\bar{X} \sim N(\mu, \sigma/\sqrt{n})$ to standard normal:

$ Z = \frac{\bar{X} - \mu}{\sigma/\sqrt{n}} \sim N(0,1) $

Use $z$ table to find CVs (e.g. $\pm 1.96$ for 95%).

Then solve for LCL/UCL:

$ LCL = \bar{x} - z_{\alpha/2} \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}} $
$ UCL = \bar{x} + z_{\alpha/2} \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}} $
I can transform $\bar{x}$ to $z$ and back
🧾 Key facts 8/8
  • CI is random; $\mu$ is fixed.
  • “95% chance interval contains $\mu$” (frequentist).
  • $n$ ↑ → E ↓ → narrower CI.
  • Confidence ↑ → wider CI.
  • Default $1-\alpha = 0.95$.

Always interpret in context of the problem.

$ \text{CI} = \bar{x} \pm E $
I can interpret a CI correctly
