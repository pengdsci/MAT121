📊 Two‑Sample CI

Means (large) CLT

Concept: both $n_1>30, n_2>30$ → normal approx.
$E = z_{\alpha/2} \sqrt{\frac{s_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{s_2^2}{n_2}}$

$(\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2) \;\pm\; z_{\alpha/2} \sqrt{\frac{s_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{s_2^2}{n_2}}$

Example: $\bar{x}_1=78, s_1=10, n_1=50$; $\bar{x}_2=82, s_2=12, n_2=100$
95% CI: $(-7.64, -0.36)$

reviewed Scenario 1
Means (known σ) exact normal

Concept: normal populations, known $\sigma_1^2,\sigma_2^2$.
$E = z_{\alpha/2} \sqrt{\frac{\sigma_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{\sigma_2^2}{n_2}}$

$(\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2) \;\pm\; z_{\alpha/2} \sqrt{\frac{\sigma_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{\sigma_2^2}{n_2}}$

Example: $\sigma_1^2=25, n_1=50$; $\sigma_2^2=16, n_2=25$
95% CI: $(-0.09, 4.09)$

reviewed Scenario 2
t‑CI (pooled) equal variance

Concept: normal, unknown equal $\sigma^2$. Use pooled $s_p^2$.
$E = t_{\alpha/2,\,n_1+n_2-2} \cdot s_p \sqrt{\frac{1}{n_1}+\frac{1}{n_2}}$

$s_p^2 = \frac{(n_1-1)s_1^2+(n_2-1)s_2^2}{n_1+n_2-2}$

Example: $n_1=25, s_1=1.8$; $n_2=20, s_2=1.5$
95% CI: $(0.09, 2.11)$

reviewed t‑distribution
Proportions normal approx

Concept: independent samples, large counts.
$E = z_{\alpha/2} \sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}_1(1-\hat{p}_1)}{n_1} + \frac{\hat{p}_2(1-\hat{p}_2)}{n_2}}$

$(\hat{p}_1 - \hat{p}_2) \;\pm\; z_{\alpha/2} \sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}_1(1-\hat{p}_1)}{n_1} + \frac{\hat{p}_2(1-\hat{p}_2)}{n_2}}$

Example: $\hat{p}_1=0.70, n_1=200$; $\hat{p}_2=0.60, n_2=150$
95% CI: $(-0.009, 0.2009)$

reviewed proportion
5‑step CI general

1. Confidence level $1-\alpha$, find $\alpha/2$
2. Check conditions → critical value (z or t)
3. Margin of error $E = CV \times SE$
4. Interval: $(\text{statistic}) \pm E$
5. Interpret in context

$\text{CI} = \text{estimate} \;\pm\; \text{CV} \cdot \text{SE}$

Always check assumptions before computing.

reviewed workflow
Conditions checklist

Means (large): $n_1>30,\; n_2>30$
Means (normal known): normal populations, known variances
t‑CI: normal, equal unknown variances
Proportions: $n\hat{p}\ge5,\; n(1-\hat{p})\ge5$ for both

Interpret: “We are $100(1-\alpha)\%$ confident that the true difference lies between …”

includes 0 → no significant difference
