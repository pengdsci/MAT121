📊 Hypothesis Testing: Logic · Components · Formulas · Examples

Claim & Hypotheses 🔑

Claim = statement about population parameter (μ or p).

H₀ (null) must contain “=”: ≤, ≥, or =.

Hₐ (alternative) never contains “=”: ≠, >, or <.

\( H_0: \mu = \mu_0 \quad \text{vs.} \quad H_a: \mu \ne \mu_0 \)
\( H_0: \mu \le \mu_0 \quad \text{vs.} \quad H_a: \mu > \mu_0 \)
\( H_0: \mu \ge \mu_0 \quad \text{vs.} \quad H_a: \mu < \mu_0 \)

💡 Example: “mean is at most 2.5” → claim \( \mu \le 2.5 \) = H₀.

reviewed
Test Statistic 📐

Converts sample statistic to a standard score (assuming H₀ true).

\( Z = \frac{\bar{x} - \mu_0}{s / \sqrt{n}} \)   (large sample, CLT)

Interpretation: standardized distance between \(\bar{x}\) and \(\mu_0\).

🧮 Example: \( n=36, \bar{x}=98.2, s=0.62, \mu_0=98.6 \)

\( Z = \frac{98.2 - 98.6}{0.62/\sqrt{36}} = -3.87 \)
reviewed
Critical Values ✂️

Rejection Region (RR): values of TS that cause rejection of H₀.

Critical value(s): cut-off(s) at significance level \(\alpha\).

Two-tailed: \( \pm z_{\alpha/2} \)  ·  Right-tailed: \( +z_{\alpha} \)  ·  Left-tailed: \( -z_{\alpha} \)

📌 Common α = 0.05 → two-tailed CV = ±1.96

📐 If TS falls in RR → reject H₀.

reviewed
Errors ⚠️

Type I error: reject H₀ when H₀ is true (probability α).

Type II error: fail to reject H₀ when H₀ is false (probability β).

\( P(\text{Type I}) = \alpha \)  ·  \( P(\text{Type II}) = \beta \)

🧪 Analogy: patient has disease, but doctor says NO disease (Type I).

reviewed
Two-tailed Test ↔️

Alternative: \( H_a: \mu \ne \mu_0 \)

RR split equally on both tails, each area \( \alpha/2 \).

Reject H₀ if \( |Z| > z_{\alpha/2} \)

📘 Example: \( H_0: \mu = 80 \) vs. \( H_a: \mu \ne 80 \), α=0.05 → CV = ±1.96

reviewed
Right-tailed Test ➡️

Alternative: \( H_a: \mu > \mu_0 \)

RR on the right tail, area = α.

Reject H₀ if \( Z > z_{\alpha} \)

📗 Example: \( H_0: \mu \le 100 \) vs. \( H_a: \mu > 100 \), α=0.05 → CV = +1.645

reviewed
Left-tailed Test ⬅️

Alternative: \( H_a: \mu < \mu_0 \)

RR on the left tail, area = α.

Reject H₀ if \( Z < -z_{\alpha} \)

📕 Example: \( H_0: \mu \ge 80 \) vs. \( H_a: \mu < 80 \), α=0.05 → CV = -1.645

reviewed
6-Step Procedure 📋
  1. Claim – identify the statement about μ or p.
  2. H₀ & Hₐ – set up null and alternative.
  3. Test statistic – compute \( Z \) (or \( t \)).
  4. Critical value(s) – based on α, define RR.
  5. Decision – reject or fail to reject H₀.
  6. Conclusion – state in context of the claim.

🧩 Always tie conclusion back to the original claim.

reviewed
Example: Body Temp 🌡️

Claim: μ = 98.6°F. Sample: n=36, \(\bar{x}=98.2\), s=0.62.

\( H_0: \mu = 98.6 \quad vs. \quad H_a: \mu \ne 98.6 \)

TS = \( \frac{98.2-98.6}{0.62/\sqrt{36}} = -3.87 \)

α=0.05 → CV = ±1.96. TS falls in RR → reject H₀.

✅ Conclusion: not enough evidence to support claim μ=98.6.

Example: Teaching Method 🎓

Claim: score changed from 80. n=36, \(\bar{x}=88\), s=10.

\( H_0: \mu = 80 \quad vs. \quad H_a: \mu \ne 80 \)

TS = \( \frac{88-80}{10/\sqrt{36}} = 4.8 \)   CV = ±1.96

TS in RR → reject H₀ → conclude score has changed.

📊 Two-tailed test, α=0.05.
