📊 Normal Distribution

Z ~ N(0,1) standard

Standard normal: mean μ = 0, σ = 1. Symmetric, total area = 1.

\( P(Z < z) = \Phi(z) \)   (left-tail area)

Example: \( P(Z < -1.48) = 0.0694 \) (from table).

key concept
Probabilities Z

Left-tail: \( P(Z < a) \) read directly from table.

Right-tail: \( P(Z > a) = 1 - P(Z < a) \)

Between: \( P(a < Z < b) = P(Z < b) - P(Z < a) \)

Example: \( P(-1.96 < Z < 0.75) = 0.7734 - 0.0250 = 0.7484 \)
methods
Percentiles Z

Find \( z_0 \) such that \( P(Z < z_0) = q \) (given left-tail area).

Use table in reverse: locate probability, read z-score.

90th percentile: \( P(Z < 1.28) \approx 0.90 \)
(closest values 0.8997 → 1.28)
inverse lookup
X ~ N(μ,σ) general

Transform to standard normal: \( Z = \frac{X - \mu}{\sigma} \)

\( P(X < x) = P\left(Z < \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma}\right) \)

Example: N(500,100): \( P(X < 600) = P(Z < 1) = 0.8413 \)

transformation
Probabilities X

Use z-score then standard normal table.

Example: N(22,4)
\( P(X > 17) = P(Z > -1.25) = 0.8944 \)

Between: \( P(25 < X < 31) = P(0.75 < Z < 2.25) = 0.2144 \)

application
Percentiles X

Find \( z_0 \) from standard normal, then \( x = \mu + z_0 \cdot \sigma \)

Example: N(500,100), 15th percentile
\( z_0 \approx -1.04 \) → \( x = 500 - 1.04(100) = 396 \)

10th percentile (warehouse): \( x = 22 - 1.28(4) = 16.88 \) ft

inverse transform
Thermometer Z

Readings ~ N(0,1). Find probabilities:

  • \( P(-1.5 < Z < 0) = 0.4332 \)
  • \( P(Z < -2.43) = 0.0075 \)
95th percentile: \( z_{0.95} \approx 1.645 \)
(average of 1.64 & 1.65)
worked example
Blood Pressure X

Diastolic ~ N(80, 8).

\( P(X > 90) = P(Z > 1.25) = 0.1056 \)

90th percentile (higher than 90%):
\( z_{0.90} = 1.28 \) → \( x = 80 + 1.28(8) = 90.24 \)
