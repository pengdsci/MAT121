📊 paired t-test

🔗 paired samples concept

Paired samples occur when each observation in one sample is paired with a specific observation in another — typically before/after or two conditions on the same subject.

\( (x_1,y_1), (x_2,y_2), \dots, (x_n,y_n) \)
same subject, two measurements

key idea reduce two‑sample problem to one‑sample by analyzing differences \( d_i = x_i - y_i \).

🧠 logic & hypotheses paired t‑test

Test if the mean difference \(\Delta = \mu_{\text{before}} - \mu_{\text{after}}\) is zero (or some value).

\( H_0: \Delta = \Delta_0 \)   vs   \( H_a: \Delta \ne \Delta_0 \) (two‑tailed)
\( H_0: \Delta \le 0 \)   vs   \( H_a: \Delta > 0 \) (right‑tailed)

assumption differences \( d_i \) are normally distributed (small samples).

📐 test statistic formula

From the differences \( d_1, d_2, \dots, d_n \):

\( \bar{d} = \frac{1}{n}\sum d_i \)   ,   \( s_d = \sqrt{\frac{\sum (d_i - \bar{d})^2}{n-1}} \)

\( \text{TS} = \dfrac{\bar{d} - \Delta_0}{s_d / \sqrt{n}} \sim t_{n-1} \)

df degrees of freedom \( = n-1 \)

🎯 critical value rejection region

Find \( t_{\alpha/2,\, n-1} \) or \( t_{\alpha,\, n-1} \) from t‑table.

two‑tailed: \( |\text{TS}| > t_{\alpha/2,\, n-1} \)
right‑tailed: \( \text{TS} > t_{\alpha,\, n-1} \)
left‑tailed: \( \text{TS} < -t_{\alpha,\, n-1} \)

example \( n=6,\ \alpha=0.05 \) right‑tail → \( t_{5,0.05}=2.015 \)

📋 6‑step procedure paired t‑test
  1. Calculate differences \( d_i \) and their \( \bar{d}, s_d \).
  2. State \( H_0 \) and \( H_a \) (based on claim).
  3. Compute TS = \( \frac{\bar{d} - \Delta_0}{s_d/\sqrt{n}} \).
  4. Find critical value(s) from \( t_{n-1} \) table.
  5. Decision: reject \( H_0 \) if TS in rejection region.
  6. Conclusion in context of the problem.
🖨️ example printer coating

Claim: coating increases operating temperature. \( d = \) coating – no‑coating.

differences: \( \{3,1,4,4,-2,2\} \)
\( \bar{d}=2,\quad s_d=2.28,\quad n=6 \)

\( H_0: \Delta \le 0 \) vs \( H_a: \Delta > 0 \)
TS = \( \frac{2 - 0}{2.28/\sqrt{6}} \approx 2.15 \)
\( t_{5,0.05}=2.015 \) → reject \( H_0 \) ✅

conclusion coating increases mean temperature.

⚠️ caution difference direction

The form of \( d_i \) (before – after or after – before) changes the alternative hypothesis.

if \( d = \) coating – no‑coating : \( H_a: \Delta > 0 \)
if \( d = \) no‑coating – coating : \( H_a: \Delta < 0 \)

Always match the sign of \( \Delta \) to the research question.

✅ when to use paired t‑test
  • same subjects measured twice (before/after, left/right).
  • matched pairs (twins, similar items).
  • differences are approximately normal (or n ≥ 30).
  • test for a mean difference.
\( \text{paired t-test} \equiv \text{one-sample t-test on differences} \)
