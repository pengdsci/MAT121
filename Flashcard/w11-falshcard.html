📊 Two‑Sample Tests

📌 Large‑Sample Z

Concept: Compare two population means when $n_1, n_2 > 30$ (or normal & known variances).

$TS = \dfrac{(\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2) - 0}{\sqrt{\frac{s_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{s_2^2}{n_2}}} \;\; \sim \; N(0,1)$

Example (left‑tailed): Texas vs Virginia daily cost.
$H_0: \mu_1 - \mu_2 \ge 0$, $H_a: \mu_1 - \mu_2 < 0$
$\bar{x}_1=248,\; \bar{x}_2=252,\; s_1=15,\; s_2=22,\; n_1=50,\; n_2=35$
$TS \approx -0.934$, $CV=-2.33$ → fail to reject $H_0$.

📌 Pooled t‑test

Concept: Two normal populations, unknown but equal variances. Pooled variance:

$s_p^2 = \dfrac{(n_1-1)s_1^2 + (n_2-1)s_2^2}{n_1 + n_2 - 2}$
$TS = \dfrac{(\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2) - 0}{\sqrt{\frac{s_p^2}{n_1} + \frac{s_p^2}{n_2}}} \;\; \sim \; t_{n_1+n_2-2}$

Example (two‑tailed): GTI vs Focus braking distances.
$\bar{x}_1=134,\; \bar{x}_2=143,\; s_1=6.9,\; s_2=6.6,\; n_1=8,\; n_2=10$
$s_p^2 = 45.33$, $TS \approx -2.818$, $df=16$, $CV=\pm 2.921$
→ fail to reject $H_0$: no difference in means.

⚙️ Workflow Summary

6‑step procedure:

  1. Claim → write in terms of $\mu_1 - \mu_2$
  2. $H_0$ and $H_a$ (left, right, or two‑tailed)
  3. Test statistic (Z or pooled t)
  4. Critical value / p‑value
  5. Decision (reject $H_0$ or not)
  6. Conclusion in context
Key: keep difference consistent: $\mu_1 - \mu_2$
📋 Assumptions

Large‑sample Z:

  • $n_1 > 30$ and $n_2 > 30$ or
  • both populations normal and variances known

Pooled t‑test:

  • both populations normal
  • variances unknown but equal
  • independent samples

⚠️ If assumptions fail, these tests are not valid.

🧪 Practice: Body Temp

Women: $n=65,\; \bar{x}=98.105,\; s=0.699$
Men: $n=65,\; \bar{x}=98.395,\; s=0.743$

Test if mean body temperatures differ (two‑tailed, $\alpha=0.05$).

$TS = \dfrac{(98.105-98.395)-0}{\sqrt{\frac{0.699^2}{65}+\frac{0.743^2}{65}}} \approx -2.28$

Critical value $\approx \pm 1.96$ → reject $H_0$: significant difference.

💳 Practice: Credit Debt

Males: $n=200,\; \bar{x}=3270,\; s=900$
Females: $n=200,\; \bar{x}=2960,\; s=850$

Claim: difference in mean debt. $\alpha=0.05$

$TS = \dfrac{(3270-2960)-0}{\sqrt{\frac{900^2}{200}+\frac{850^2}{200}}} \approx 3.57$

$p$-value $< 0.001$ → reject $H_0$: strong evidence of difference.

📱 Pooled t: Phone Range

Manufacturer: $n_1=14,\; \bar{x}_1=1270,\; s_1=25$
Competitor: $n_2=16,\; \bar{x}_2=1250,\; s_2=30$

Claim: manufacturer range greater (right‑tailed), $\alpha=0.05$

$s_p^2 = \frac{13(25^2)+15(30^2)}{28} \approx 774.11$
$TS = \frac{1270-1250}{\sqrt{774.11/14 + 774.11/16}} \approx 1.87$

$t_{28,0.05} \approx 1.701$ → reject $H_0$: support manufacturer's claim.

📐 Formula Summary

Large‑sample Z:

$Z = \dfrac{(\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2)}{\sqrt{\frac{s_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{s_2^2}{n_2}}}$

Pooled t:

$s_p^2 = \frac{(n_1-1)s_1^2 + (n_2-1)s_2^2}{n_1+n_2-2}$
$t = \dfrac{(\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2)}{\sqrt{s_p^2(\frac{1}{n_1}+\frac{1}{n_2})}}$

df $= n_1+n_2-2$
